A Survey of Embodied AI: From Simulators to Research Tasks (April 2022)

In this episode we unpack a survey by Jiafei Duan, Samson Yu, Hui Li Tan, Hongyuan Zhu, and Cheston Tan from Nanyang Technological University and A*STAR's Institute for Infocomm Research, published in IEEE Transactions on Emerging Topics in Computational Intelligence. The argument: the field has shifted from "internet AI," where agents learn from curated images, text, and video, to "embodied AI," where agents learn by interacting with environments from an egocentric viewpoint similar to humans, and the simulator is now the central piece of research infrastructure for that transition.

We walk through the nine simulators the paper benchmarks (DeepMind Lab, AI2-THOR, CHALET, VirtualHome, VRKitchen, Habitat-Sim, iGibson, SAPIEN, and ThreeDWorld), the seven technical features used to compare them (Environment, Physics, Object Type, Object Property, Controller, Action, Multi-Agent) rolled up into the secondary axes of Realism, Scalability, and Interactivity, and the distinction between game-based and world-based scene construction. We then cover the pyramid of research tasks that sit on top of those simulators — Visual Exploration, Visual Navigation (Point Navigation, Object Navigation, Vision-and-Language Navigation), and Embodied Question Answering — along with the methods they rely on (PPO, DD-PPO, SplitNet, Neural SLAM, scene memory transformers), the evaluation metrics that pin them down (SPL, Success Rate, ATV), and the Sim2Real benchmarks like the iGibson Sim2Real Challenge, the Habitat Challenge, and RoboTHOR, and close on the gap between simulated performance and real-world robotics.

Reference: Duan, J., Yu, S., Tan, H. L., Zhu, H., & Tan, C. (2022). A Survey of Embodied AI: From Simulators to Research Tasks. IEEE Transactions on Emerging Topics in Computational Intelligence, 6(2), 230–244. https://doi.org/10.1109/TETCI.2022.3141105
